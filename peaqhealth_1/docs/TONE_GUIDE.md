# Peaq Health — Tone & Language Guide

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** April 2026  
**Applies to:** All user-facing copy — marker pages, connection lines, 
article content, dashboard text, AI-generated narratives, action items, 
onboarding, empty states, CTAs, tooltips, error messages.

---

## The Core Principle

**Lead with consequence. Not mechanism.**

A user does not need to know what NF-kB is.  
They need to know that inflammation is quietly aging their blood.

The science belongs one tap away — in the collapsed section, the article,  
the sources list. The surface is always plain English.

---

## The Person We Are Writing For

Imagine a 34-year-old who is genuinely interested in their health,  
reads broadly, but is not a scientist or clinician. They are smart.  
They are busy. They want to understand what is happening in their body  
and what to do about it — without having to Google every other word.

They will leave if you make them feel talked down to.  
They will also leave if you make them feel lost.

Write for that person. Every time.

---

## The Voice

**Calm. Direct. Specific. Never alarming.**

Peaq is a scientist friend who happens to know your results.  
Not a doctor reading from a chart. Not a wellness influencer.  
A knowledgeable friend who gives you real information in plain language  
and tells you what they would actually do in your situation.

---

## Rules

### 1. Plain English first. Science second.

**Wrong:**  
"NF-kB pathway activation drives CRP upregulation downstream of  
periodontal pathogen translocation."

**Right:**  
"Bacteria from your gums enter your bloodstream briefly every time  
you eat or brush. Your immune system responds by producing more  
inflammation. That shows up as elevated CRP."

If you need a technical term, define it immediately in the same sentence.  
Never use a technical term twice without defining it first.

---

### 2. Consequence before mechanism

Lead with what it means for the person.  
Then explain why, if space allows.

**Wrong:**  
"Neisseria species reduce dietary nitrate to nitrite via enzymatic  
reduction, which is subsequently converted to nitric oxide."

**Right:**  
"The bacteria responsible for this conversion are depleted in your  
oral panel. That means the nitrate from your vegetables is not  
doing what it should."

---

### 3. Specific over vague

**Wrong:**  
"Inflammation can affect many aspects of your health."

**Right:**  
"Elevated CRP directly adds years to your Peaq Age. Getting it  
below 1.0 mg/L is achievable — most people can move the needle  
within 4 to 8 weeks."

Numbers, timeframes, specific foods, specific products — always  
more useful than generalities.

---

### 4. Action over observation

Every piece of content should end with something the person can do.  
Not a vague suggestion. A specific, ranked action.

**Wrong:**  
"Lifestyle changes may help reduce inflammation."

**Right:**  
"The single highest-leverage move: anchor your bedtime within a  
30-minute window every night, including weekends. CRP responds  
within 2 to 4 weeks."

---

### 5. Never catastrophize. Never minimize.

Do not use alarming language for findings that are common and addressable.  
Do not use dismissive language for findings that genuinely warrant attention.

**Wrong (alarming):**  
"Your inflammation is dangerously elevated and may indicate serious  
cardiovascular risk."

**Wrong (dismissive):**  
"Your CRP is slightly elevated but nothing to worry about."

**Right:**  
"Your CRP is in the moderate range — above the optimal threshold  
but common and addressable. Here is what moves it."

---

### 6. No jargon without immediate translation

**Banned on the surface (dashboard, connection lines, dot tooltips):**
- NF-kB, eNOS, RMSSD, PhenoAge, OMA
- Periodontitis, dysbiosis, bacteremia
- Nitrate-nitrite-nitric oxide pathway
- Volatile sulfur compounds
- DNRA organisms
- 16S rRNA sequencing
- Gram-negative, anaerobic, commensal
- Cytokines, IL-6, TNF-alpha
- Cardiometabolic, endothelial

**Allowed with immediate plain-language translation:**
- "hs-CRP (an inflammation marker)"
- "HbA1c (a 3-month blood sugar average)"
- "Neisseria (the bacteria that help produce nitric oxide)"
- "Shannon diversity (how many different species are present)"

**Allowed without translation in articles (collapsed science section only):**
Anything with a citation. The science section is for people who want depth.

---

### 7. Species names: never on the surface

Never show Latin species names on the dashboard, connection lines,  
dot tooltips, or action items.

**Wrong:**  
"Porphyromonas gingivalis elevated — linked to systemic inflammation."

**Right:**  
"Harmful bacteria elevated — linked to inflammation throughout the body."

Species names belong in: article science sections, collapsed "learn more"  
sections, PDF reports for clinical users.

**Translation guide:**
| Species name | Plain English |
|---|---|
| Neisseria, Rothia | "the bacteria that produce nitric oxide" |
| Porphyromonas, Fusobacterium | "the bacteria linked to gum disease" |
| Streptococcus mutans | "cavity-associated bacteria" |
| Solobacterium, Fusobacterium (VSC) | "odor-associated bacteria" |
| Peptostreptococcus | "harmful bacteria" |
| Prevotella melaninogenica | "competing bacteria" |

---

### 8. Favorable findings matter as much as unfavorable ones

Do not only surface problems. Surfacing what is working builds trust  
and motivates the user to protect what is good.

---

### 9. The "friend test"

Before publishing any copy, ask: would a knowledgeable friend say this?

A friend would not say "NF-kB pathway activation."  
A friend would not say "lifestyle modifications may be beneficial."  
A friend would say "stop the mouthwash — it's probably the fastest  
thing you can do here, and it's free."

If it sounds like a medical pamphlet or a wellness influencer,  
rewrite it.

---

### 10. Peaq-specific framing

These phrases are on-brand:
- "Only Peaq can see this connection"
- "No single test can show this"
- "Here is what that means for you"
- "This is what Peaq is built to catch"
- "The fix is not [X]. It is [Y]."
- "Most people never hear this from their doctor"

These phrases are off-brand:
- "Optimize your wellness journey"
- "Unlock your health potential"
- "Holistic approach to wellbeing"
- "Listen to your body"
- "Superfood"

## Red-Flag Words (Never Use on the Surface)

| Banned | Use instead |
|---|---|
| Dysbiosis | "imbalance in your oral bacteria" |
| Microbiome (standalone) | "the bacteria in your mouth / gut" |
| Biomarker | "marker" or just name the marker |
| Optimize / optimization | "improve" |
| Holistic | (don't use) |
| Protocol | "plan" or "routine" |
| Inflammatory cascade | "inflammation" |
| Endothelial function | "blood vessel health" |
| Autonomic nervous system | "your body's stress response system" |
| Parasympathetic | "rest and recovery mode" |
| Sympathetic dominance | "stress response running too high" |
| Circadian rhythm | "your body's internal clock" (first use) |
| Glycemic variability | "blood sugar swings" |
| Insulin sensitivity | "how well your body handles sugar" |
| Cardiometabolic | "heart and metabolic health" |
